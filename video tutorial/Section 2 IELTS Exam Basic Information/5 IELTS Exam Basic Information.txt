Again, the Key Changes to IELTS in 2026 [The exam is the SAME]

· Phase-out of Paper-Based Tests: From mid-2026, the traditional paper-based IELTS test will be phased out globally.

· Move to Computer-Delivered IELTS: Almost all IELTS tests will be delivered on a computer at test centers.

· "Writing on Paper" Option: In select locations, a "Writing on Paper" option will be introduced, allowing candidates to handwrite the Writing component while taking Listening and Reading on a computer.

· Wider Availability of One Skill Retake: The 'One Skill Retake' option—which allows candidates to retake a single section (e.g., Writing) without retaking the entire test—will be standard on computer-based tests.

· Faster Results: Computer-based tests will provide results faster, often within 1–2 days.

· "Black Pen" Rule: In some remaining paper-based tests leading up to the transition, candidates may be required to use black pens instead of pencils.

· Increased Use of Video Call Speaking (VCS): While the speaking test remains face-to-face, many centers are transitioning to conducting the test via a live video call with an examiner in a separate room

What Is NOT Changing

· Test Difficulty and Format: The overall test structure (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking), question types, and scoring system (0–9 band scale) remain exactly the same.

· Global Recognition: Universities, governments, and organisations continue to recognize the same quality and standards.

· Result Validity: Current IELTS results will remain valid for the standard two-year period
